Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic shows the easy guide to the modern world tech in three sections, which is specially tailored for students. 
The three section is:-

a)Defining digital literacy:
Digital literacy is more than just knowing how to use a computer. The graphic defines it as the complete skillset required to find and 
evaluate information online and create and communicate ideas effectively using digital tools.

b)Essential Tools for students that help them to stay organized and productive:
Google Calendar: For time management.
Cloud Storage: To save and access files from anywhere.
Note-taking Apps: To write the ideas and study more efficiently.
Zoom: To stay connected with classmates and teachers.

c)Safe internet Practice that helps them to protect their digital data like:
Password Security: Use strong passwords that mix letters, numbers, and symbols.
Always "think before you click" by verifying links and senders.
Privacy: Protect personal information and share only what is necessary.
Control your passwords and capture links and senders.

The Takeaway: The infographic emphasizes that being "tech-savy" isn't just about speed—it's about being resourceful, organized, and secure.
